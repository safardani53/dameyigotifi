CORONATION Street viewers will be left disappointed tonight (January 27, 2023) as the soap won't be on.

Here's why the ITV soap won't be airing in its usual slot.

Why is Coronation Street not on tonight?

Coronation Street is not on tonight (January 27, 2023) as ITV make way for a football match.

Instead of the soap being on, it will be replaced by Manchester City's FA Cup fourth round game with Arsenal, with kick-off set for 8pm.

However, Corrie fans haven't missed out as an hour-long episode aired on Thursday, February 2, 2023, instead.

TV shake-ups have been frequent with Corrie fans having to wait several days before being able to catch up.

The soap pulled off the air for the World Cup in December 2022, and the changes also had an impact on rival ITV show Emmerdale and BBC One's EastEnders.

When is Coronation Street back on?

Coronation Street will be back to its regular schedule on Monday, January 30, 2023, airing from 8pm.

The show airs three, one-hour episodes every week.

This means you can watch it Mondays, Wednesday and Fridays on ITV at 8pm.

When did Coronation Street first air?

Coronation Street was first broadcast on December 9, 1960.

It was created by Tony Warren, and pitched as a "kitchen sink drama serial" about the domestic lives of the working class in the north of England.

The soap focuses specifically on the residents of Coronation Street in the fictional town of Weatherfield in Greater Manchester.

Since 1960 it has been produced by Granada Television and for most of that time it has been one of the UK's highest rated shows.

Fans love Corrie for its mixture of drama and comedy, usually shying away from the sensationalist and far-fetched plots associated with the soap genre.

The soap focuses on the lives of the residents of Weatherfield, and has some iconic buildings, including the Rovers Return and Rita's Corner Shop.
