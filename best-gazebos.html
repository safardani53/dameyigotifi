WITH spring upon us and warmer weather (hopefully) around the corner, our thoughts turn to spending more time outdoors – and the best garden gazebos enable us to do just that.

There’s something about eating al fresco, enjoying a glass of wine with friends outside, or simply relaxing in the garden that is part of summer.

And, even though we can’t always rely on great weather in the UK, if you've got a gazebo that doesn’t matter so much.

Plus, if the weather is scorching, then the best gazebos will also protect you from the sun’s rays.

There are many types of gazebo available, from compact pop-up styles perfect for travel, to high-end sturdy wooden gazebos that won't budge; the choice is vast.

A stylish, statement way to spruce up your outdoor area, gazebos are also an excellent way to extend your entertainment space for lawn parties and BBQs, allowing guests to enjoy alfresco dining and socialising while sheltering from the sun, wind and rain.

When it comes to budget, gazebos are available at every price point. Simple, affordable designs start at around £50 and, depending on the material and size of gazebo you're after, at the top end of the market, permanent gazebos can reach into the thousands.

Extras to look out for when purchasing your gazebo are built-in LED lights, pockets and shelving, which are ideal for outdoor grilling.

Whatever you need your gazebo for – whether as an extra shelter during camping, a place to enjoy outdoor dining, a designated garden play space for kids, or as an all-weather cover for your hot tub – we've got it covered with our selection of the best garden gazebos available to buy right now.

What is the best brand of gazebo?

The best brand of gazebo will depend on what you're using it for.

High street retailers such as Argos are good if you're looking for a good quality, well-priced model.

There are also many lesser-known brands with good ratings and reviews on Amazon, which can be a good option if you're looking for reliable, tried and tested products.

What is the best heavy-duty gazebo?

The best heavy-duty gazebo will be a permanent one made out of wood or metal.

Lighter, pop-up gazebos can last for a few years but they have to be weighed down, and may also become easily ripped and damaged when you're putting them up or folding them away.

A permanent, fixed gazebo won't move and will ensure your chosen area stays dry and safe, although it may need more care to keep it in top shape for years to come.

What is the easiest gazebo to put up?

The easiest gazebo to put up will be a pop-up one.

Some will open out with no assembly required — they do need to be weighed down to save from blowing away in windier conditions, though.

You can also get metal framed ones that fold out but they're heavier and will need two or three people to set up.

How to build a gazebo

Gazebos come in all shapes and sizes, with each having its own specific sets of instructions.

Usually, though, gazebos will need at least two people to help set them up. Pop-up ones are definitely easier to build, often having a similar opening and closing system to an umbrella.

We recommend starting by building the (often steel) frames for the roof first, then moving onto the legs, which often extend with a knob or screw.

Then, place the roof on top of the legs and extend all the legs, with the roof in place, to your most suitable height.

How to secure a gazebo from wind

Most gazebos come with weight bags, ropes or stakes to keep them strong and secure in windy conditions. Make sure to do this, as the brand is unlikely to refund you if it blows away after being badly secured.

Often, gazebo legs have 'feet' with holes in to secure a stake through. Ropes hook from the stakes, up through the fabric covers and to the roof, keeping even the walls in place.

We recommend putting your gazebo on flat, sturdy ground that won't flood and sink.

Do I need planning permission for a gazebo?

Generally, you don't need planning permission for a gazebo in the UK, if it's temporary (often erected for less than a month) and no larger than around 2-2.5m in height.

You aren't allowed to erect a gazebo at the front of your house according to your Permitted Development Rights - so it'll have to be in your back garden or yard.

However, if you live in a protected area, or are planning to build a large gazebo structure that is heavy-duty, you will likely need planning permission from your local council.

If you're unsure, we would recommend getting in touch with your local council representative.

Is a gazebo worth it?

A gazebo is a good investment if you want to spend time in the garden come rain or shine, and a well-made one can last for years.

If you're prone to sunburn or don't like the heat, it'll provide you with shade. And if the weather turns, some will also protect you from the rain.

Plus, if you get one with mesh sides, you can dine al fresco without worrying about pesky bugs interrupting.

Just bear in mind that you'll need to have a fair bit of space in your garden and somewhere to store it when not in use.
