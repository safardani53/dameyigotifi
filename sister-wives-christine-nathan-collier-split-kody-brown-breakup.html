NATHAN and Christine Collier, who shared their family drama on Sister Wives, have split after friend Kody Brown's breakup from his three wives, The U.S. Sun can exclusively reveal.

The Colliers made an appearance on the hit TLC show in season 8. After a three-year battle in the US District Court, fighting for the right to legally marry second wife Christine, Nathan is now still married to Victoria "Vicki" Collier.

"I can confirm that yes we have split at the end of July of this year," Christine, 46, told The U.S. Sun exclusively.

This came after Kody confirmed his splits from Janelle and his third wife Christine during the Sister Wives One on One: Part 1 special. 

Christine, citing "irreconcilable differences" as the grounds for the split, added: "It's personal. I'd rather not air that information (why they split)."

THREE-YEAR COURT BATTLE

Nathan, 53, and Vicki, 49, were legally married in Dillon, South Carolina, on April 26, 2000. In 2007, he tied the knot to Christine in a religious ceremony, without signing a marriage license.

On June 30, 2015, Nathan attempted to apply for a marriage license to his second wife at the Yellowstone County Clerk of District Court in Montana, but his application was denied.

His court filing to amend the state's anti-polygamy laws made national news at the time following their appearance on Sister Wives where they opened up about their polygamous lifestyle. 

Nathan, who's still based in Billings, Montana, was hoping to make a change after the Supreme Court's ruling on same-sex marriage that took place that same month. 

In March 2018, after three years of arguing in court, the judge rejected Nathan and his wives' case to overturn the state's anti-polygamy laws.

In the court document obtained by The U.S. Sun, United States Magistrate Judge Timothy J. Cavan concluded: "Even after giving the Colliers the benefit of the substantial doubt that they have formulated a concrete plan to violate Mont. Code Ann. §§ 45-5-611 or 612, the Court finds that the Colliers have failed to demonstrate injury in fact because they have not shown a threat of prosecution or a history of enforcement, and therefore they do not have standing to challenge those criminal statutes."

The Colliers had 14 days to file written objections of the judge's findings and recommendations, but never went through with it. 

'UNEXPECTED BREAKUP'

Christine, who helped parent eight children jointly with the Colliers, said that she's now in a "new relationship," but did not want to disclose more information on her life today or share more about her former spiritual marriage.

She said that she had learned about the Sister Wives' split through a friend, and told The U.S. Sun: "I was not friends with them, nor did we keep close afterward [the show].

"Whatever is going on in their family ... it's their business. I try to stay out of other people's business." 

In the Sister Wives' 2015 episode that featured the Colliers, the Montana-based family hosted the Browns at their home where they discussed their polygamist lifestyles and had fun on the property riding ATVs with their kids. 

The Colliers talked about their plans to build a duplex, having separate kitchens but a shared basement as a common living room for the family.

Vicki said in the episode: "We want an even balance. We would like a little more space, autonomy, but at the same time, will not compromise our unity."

Christine added: "Our children thrive on that unity. They need that unity. We are one unit. Even though we are two different marriages and two separate marriages, we're one unit."

A source told The U.S. Sun: "Everyone was pining for Nathan to have a [TV] series because he had such a great family. His family's breakup is completely unexpected."

FUTURE OF POLYGAMY

Kody, 53, recently shared insight on his past marriages to Meri, Christine and Janelle.

For one, Kody - whose fourth wife is Robyn- claimed that Christine said that she was in a "loveless marriage" and called her a "manipulator" in the first episode of the tell-all that aired on Sunday, December 18.

"All these polygamous families are falling apart, and the men are starting to see a pattern," an insider shared.

"To have these women mock and do what they did publicly to Kody, gave these other women the power and the courage to walk away as well. It's a big deal in the polygamy world right now.

"The truth of the matter is, there's a whole betrayal going on. Families are divided and broken up."

The source added: "Sister Wives started as bringing positive awareness to polygamy, not ruin this generation." 

The U.S. Sun previously reported that despite the Brown's breakup, "it's not the end of Sister Wives."
