FOR many, the formation of the Premier League will feel like just a few years ago, for the younger generations, it's all they've ever known.

Whichever bracket you fall under, the Prem is celebrating its 25th birthday in the 2016-17 season.

From Blackburn Rovers as champions to, well, Leicester City as champions with the 'Big Four' era of domination in the middle, it's been action-packed from 1992 to present day.

To celebrate the milestone, SunSport is combining an all-time, Premier League best XI - asking you to cast the final vote.

Every day, we will be bringing you a new position and asking you to pick your favourite of our final ten in a series of polls.

At the end of it all, we'll bring you the results; the definitive SunSport Premier League XI from 1992 to 2016.

Today, we'll bring you our list of left-backs... Let's check out the contenders.

STUART PEARCE

Club(s): Nottingham Forest, Newcastle, West Ham

Appearances: 202

Premier League titles: 0

MOST remembered for that scream for England against Spain at Euro 1996, Pearce enjoyed a long career in club football.

Bursting onto the scene pre-Premier League, the left-back did muster over 200 appearances in the new era with Nottingham Forest, Newcastle and West Ham.

In fact, Pearce mustered eight seasons in the Prem, leaving following his two years at West Ham in 2001.

GRAEME LE SAUX

Club(s): Blackburn Rovers, Chelsea, Southampton

Appearances: 327

Premier League titles: 1

LE SAUX went through something of a to-me, to-you between Blackburn and Chelsea in the early 90s, kicking off his career with Rovers in 1992, before moving to the Blues the next season.

After one year at Stamford Bridge, he returned to Blackburn for four season - where he won the Premier League in 1994-95 - before moving back to Chelsea for a further six seasons.

Le Saux racked up an impressive 327 appearances, including two seasons at Southampton, as he played in the English top flight for an incredible 14 years.

NIGEL WINTERBURN

Club(s): Arsenal, West Ham

Appearances: 352

Premier League titles: 1

WINTERBURN was part of a fearsome back four - or sometimes five - alongside Tony Adams, Steve Bould, Martin Keown and Lee Dixon during the 90s at Arsenal.

The England man was a regular at Highbury for all eight seasons he spent in north London, including winning the league title in the 1997-98 season.

Following his time at the Gunners, Winterburn spent three seasons at West Ham, as he racked up a career total of 352 Prem appearances.

DENIS IRWIN

Club(s): Manchester United, Wolverhampton Wanderers

Appearances: 328

Premier League titles: 7

SEVEN Premier League titles, a superb, set-piece specialist and over 300 appearances to his name.

Irish full-back Irwin will go down in history as one of the greats, as he was a regular in the Manchester United side that dominated the 90s.

Winning seven Prem trophies in just ten seasons at Old Trafford is simply mind-boggling.

IAN HARTE

Club(s): Leeds, Sunderland, Reading

Appearances: 237

Premier League titles: 0

BEFORE there was Ashley Cole, Harte made the left-back position glamorous, with an attack-minded approach and a lethal left foot.

After all, not many defenders can boast 28 goals in the Premier League - after netting over three goals per season during nine terms with Leeds.

A season apiece at Sunderland and Reading in the top flight helped boost Harte's appearance count to 237 - with his 2012-13 Royals stint in the Prem coming five years after his previous top flight showing with the Black Cats in 2007-08.

ASHLEY COLE

Club(s): Arsenal, Chelsea

Appearances: 385

Premier League titles: 3

COLE spent eight seasons at Arsenal and a further eight at London rivals Chelsea.

During his 16 years in the top flight, Cole made an incredible 385 appearances, including winning three Premier League titles - including one with Arsenal's 'Invincibles' in 2003-04.

93 clean sheets, 15 goals and 23 assists easily cement his place as one of the best left-backs of his generation.

JOHN ARNE RIISE

Club(s): Liverpool, Fulham

Appearances: 321

Premier League titles: 0

WHILE Riise never got his hands on the Premier League title, the Norweigan's sweet left boot will go down in history.

The former Liverpool and Fulham man spent a decade in the top flight, including seven seasons with the Reds, where he helped the Anfield side win the Champions League in 2005.

Riise notched an impressive 321 appearances with 21 goals... Normally screamers at that.

WAYNE BRIDGE

Club(s): Southampton, Chelsea, Fulham, Manchester City, West Ham, Sunderland

Appearances: 315

Premier League titles: 1

AFTER 16 years in the Premier League, Bridge's lack of appearances for England was clearly just pure bad luck having been born into the same era as Ashley Cole.

He racked up 315 Prem appearances and even got his hands on the top flight crown with Chelsea in the 2004-05 season.

During his career, Bridge turned out for Southampton, the Blues, Fulham, Manchester City, West Ham and Sunderland.

PATRICE EVRA

Club(s): Manchester United

Appearances: 273

Premier League titles: 5

THE French full-back spent nine seasons in the Premier League with Manchester United, getting his hands on the title a whopping five times.

Left-back Evra was a first team regular for his entire Prem career before he swapped Old Trafford for Juventus in 2014.

273 appearances, 97 clean sheets, 21 assists, seven goals, five titles... That is some career.

LEIGHTON BAINES

Club(s): Wigan, Everton

Appearances: 357

Premier League titles: 0

WHILE Baines is another top player to never win the Premier League, he has been a key figure in the league for well over a decade.

After two seasons at Wigan, Baines earned a move to Everton in 2007 - where he has been ever since.

With 357 appearances, 47 assists and 28 goals from that gifted left boot, the Toffees man fully justifies his spot in the top ten left-backs.

Players such as Paul Konchesky, Phil Neville and Celestine Babayaro all got close to a spot...
