Lori Greiner and the rest of the Sharks are back for Shark Tank season 15 on September 29. The hit series has become a staple on ABC’s lineup and made the Sharks household names. But what happens when the “Queen of QVC” leaves the Shark Tank?

Lori, 53, is very private about her personal life, but she has been happily married to her husband, Dan Greiner. They started dating after meeting while Lori was in college before officially tying the knot. So, who is Dan Greiner? Scroll down to learn more about Lori’s love who stays out of the spotlight.

Who Is Lori Greiner’s Husband?

Lori’s husband, Dan, plays a crucial role in her business. When Lori was inspired to create her acrylic jewelry organizer early on in her career, Dan was supportive every step of the way. “He’s cautious — not at all the type to push the envelope,” Lori wrote in her book, Invent it, Sell it, Bank it!: Make your Million-Dollar Idea into a Reality. “And yet, in this instance, he was on board from the minute I told him about my idea.”

Dan was by her side when Lori took out a $120,000 loan against their house to manufacture her jewelry organizer. When Lori launched her QVC show, orders for her jewelry organizer took off. At the time, Dan was a controller for a manufacturing company. He eventually left his job to work alongside Lori as she started her company.

The couple shared a desk at their Chicago office. “I am amazed at their relationship,” an employee told Crain’s Chicago Business about Lori and Dan. “I love my husband, but I’d kill him if I had to be with him 24/7.”

What Does Dan Greiner Do for a Living?

After leaving his former job, Dan became the vice president of Lori’s company, For Your Ease Only, Inc. “Dan is a numbers guy; I’m the inventor. That’s why we’re a perfect team,” Lori wrote in her book.

How Did Lori Greiner Meet Her Husband?

While attending Loyola University in Chicago, Lori met Dan at a local sports bar. They dated for several years before getting married in 2010, according to Closer.

Does Lori Greiner Have Kids?

Lori and Dan do not have children. Lori is dedicated to her career as an entrepreneur and believes that involving family, like her husband, in her business is one of the keys to her success.

“The truth is you should try to involve your family in your business,” she told Young Hollywood in 2016. “That’s what I think. [It] works for me. I try to involve the people I love in what I’m doing, and therefore they’re going to be a part of the journey and be excited, and they’re not going to mind that I’m gone because they’ll come with me.”
