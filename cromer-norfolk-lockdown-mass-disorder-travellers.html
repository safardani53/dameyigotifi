Where is Cromer, why was the Norfolk town on lockdown and did travellers cause the mass disord

April 01, 2024
blog

TRAVELLERS were evicted from a sleepy seaside town after it was placed on lockdown amid scenes of mass disorder.

Cromer in Norfolk was plunged into chaos as police were called to a series of incidents on August 19. We reveal all...

Where is Cromer?

The seaside resort, famous for the Cromer crab, is on the north coast of Norfolk.

It is around 23 miles north of Norwich and four miles east of Sheringham on the North Sea coastline.

There are 7,683 people living in the town according to the 2011 Census.

Cromer was hosting its annual carnival week, which draws in around 250,000 people, when the trouble erupted.

What happened in Cromer?

The town was placed on "lockdown" on the weekend of August 19 after disorder forced pubs and restaurants to shut.

Venues closed for the "safety of customers and staff" following a spate of thefts.

Police were called to a series of incidents and extra patrols were in place because of "low level disorder".

There were reports of a stabbing, which police said were unfounded, but there had been thefts from shops and pubs.

Cops were also called to reports of groups refusing to leave pubs and not paying for drinks at an Indian restaurant.

A number of pubs in the town decided to close at around 7pm, with locals describing on social media how the town had gone into “lockdown”.

Businesses in Cromer have have now reopened after the disturbances.

The most serious reported crime was the rape of a teenage girl on August 18 at a bus stop on Cadogan Road by two men.

A man in his 30s was arrested in Essex on suspicion of rape.

From Friday afternoon through to Sunday, police recorded 37 incidents in Cromer.

What happened at Masala Twist in Cromer?

Indian restaurant owner Nashim Uddin claims he was invaded by more than 40 travellers in the under-siege seaside town.

Nashim Uddin says the gang frightened and intimidated customers and plundered free drinks from his popular eaterie on August 19.

The Masala Twist was one of the few places still open in Cromer on Saturday night as shops, pubs and other food outlets shut early.

Mr Uddin vowed: “I wouldn’t let people like that intimidate me into closing.”

The restaurant boss said he began pushing the thugs out backed up by his nephew Jamil Ahmed and wife Ayrun Nessa-Uddin.

He said his scared wife had her arm slammed in the door during the dramatic bust-up.

Her arm is bruised and swollen and she’s been unable to sleep properly since she was attacked by the pack.

Who was responsible for the trouble in Cromer?

A statement from authorities in the wake of the drama revealed they had evicted 23 traveller caravans that had pitched up at the same time illegally.

Deputy Chief Constable Nick Dean said: "An influx of the travelling community in Cromer led to an increase in low level disorder and thefts and the events that we know about.

“What I want to be really careful of is not labelling the whole of the travelling community in terms of this disorder.”

Local authority Leader Tom FitzPatrick said: “The district council served notice on the travellers to leave on Saturday and all the travellers left the site on Sunday.

“North Norfolk provides temporary stopping places for travellers and the Council will always act swiftly where there are illegal encampments.

“Both I and officers have been out and about speaking to businesses in Cromer to make sure we do all we can for the community.”

North Norfolk MP Norman Lamb said: "Certainly from the people I've talked to in the town, what happened yesterday was pretty intolerable and it is really important that the police deal very firmly with the perpetrators of this."
