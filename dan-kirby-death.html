RACING Driver and team owner Dan Kirby has tragically died.

Here we take a look into life, career and death of the Chelmsford racing driver and the family he leaves behind.

When did Dan Kirby die?

Dan Kirby passed away on Tuesday, September 19, 2023.

His cause of death remains unknown at the time of writing.

Following his passing, a spokesperson for the British Touring Car Championship (BTCC) took to social media and wrote: ''We are deeply saddened to hear of the passing of former BTCC driver and team owner, Dan Kirby.

"A hugely popular figure the paddock, Dan will be sorely missed.

''Dan was a huge part of our BTCC journey and he will be sorely missed."

Who was Dan Kirby?

Dan Kirby was a racing driver and team owner, who founded the Trade Price Cars Racing team in Chelmsford and was a familiar face on the touring car circuit.

The team competed in the 2019 and 2020 Kwik Fit British Touring Car Championship Seasons.

The Essex-based racing driver also ran a used car business, before taking the driver's seat in 2020 with Power Maxed Racing.

In 2021, Dan finished third in the TCR-UK Touring Car Championship at Donington Park.

He competed in the opening two rounds of this year's TCR UK championship but left ahead of the third event in June.

Was Dan Kirby married and did he have children?

As mentioned above, according to a statement shared by the BTCC on social media, Dan Kirby was married to wife Beth.
