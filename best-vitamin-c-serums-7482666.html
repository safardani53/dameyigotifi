Things to Consider Before Buying Vitamin C Serum

Concentration Level

Most everyone can benefit from layering vitamin C into their routine, whether it be vitamin C serum or in an eye cream for dark circles. So what concentration of vitamin C should you look for? Stick with a 10 percent concentration if this is your first experience with the antioxidant or if you have more sensitive or reactive skin. You can always work your way up. Our top pick, L'Oreal Revitalift Vitamin C Serum, has a 10 percent vitamin C concentration. Formulas with 15 to 20 percent vitamin C are considered a higher dose.

Skin Type

While most skin types can benefit from adding vitamin C serums to their skincare routine, if your skin is reactive or sensitive, Chung says to avoid high-concentration formulas that may cause irritation. Some vitamin C products are formulated specifically for sensitive skin, like SkinCeuticals C E Ferulic. If you have dry skin, try Sephora Collection’s Ultra Glow Serum, our top pick for dry skin.

Packaging

Vitamin C serum that is packaged in a dark glass bottle or opaque plastic tube will last longer than one stored in clear packaging. It’s also important to store your vitamin C serum in a cool, dark place to avoid UV exposure, which can make it less effective. The Klur Brilliant Light Multi-Correctional Repair Serum, which is in a dark glass bottle, and our best overall, L'Oreal Paris Revitalift Vitamin C Serum, in an opaque tube, are examples of the ideal packaging for vitamin C serums.

Other Ingredients

Vitamin C is usually thoughtfully formulated with complementary ingredients. The reason for this? "Vitamin C products often need oiler vehicles to maintain stability," Chung tells PEOPLE. However, she notes this can be problematic for some skin types (think: acne-prone skin). But luckily, brand founders and chemists are considering a range of skincare needs. So if you are acne-prone, seek formulas with squalane and rosehip oils, as they are non-comedogenic. Vitamin C can also be quite effective in hyaluronic acid serums.

As far as the actual vitamin C goes, Chung recommends products that use L-ascorbic acid as its active ingredient, preferably with vitamin E, for the best stability. This is because L-ascorbic acid is the most potent and effective form of vitamin C. Our best daytime serum, Drunk Elephant C-Firma Fresh Vitamin C Day Serum, contains both L-ascorbic acid and vitamin E.

Retinol Products

If your skin is not sensitive, you can apply both vitamin C and retinol at night. "If I'm going to use either retinol or vitamin C, I'll take retinol," Chung shares. This pairing can be a dynamic duo that gets you a little more bang for your buck if your skin isn't prone to irritation. "Retinol is your gold standard for increasing collagen and improving the turnover of skin cells while Vitamin C acts as a supporting cast," she explains. Prone to irritation? No problem. Instead, stick with vitamin C for the daytime and a retinol formula at night.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What does vitamin C serum do for your face?

    Vitamin C is clinically proven to protect against free radical damage caused by pollution, sunlight, and even alcohol while effectively evening skin tone and promoting collagen production — making it a popular active ingredient in eye serums and other skincare products. It also boosts radiance, decreases the appearance of dark spots, and leaves the skin feeling soft and smooth.

  • How should I use vitamin C serum?

    There is one must when using vitamin C (and caring for your skin in general): SPF. "I recommend using vitamin C every morning before sunscreen," Los Angeles-based celebrity esthetician Shani Darden tells PEOPLE. "During the day, you want to protect your skin while you're out and exposed to environmental stressors. The combo of vitamin C serum and sunscreen is perfect for daytime protection." It's also a good idea to seek out vitamin C in blue light skincare products. Among her favorite vitamin C-infused serums is iS Clinical Pro-Heal Serum. She notes it's an efficacious yet nourishing serum formulated with L-ascorbic acid, a pure and potent form of vitamin C. It is also usually well-tolerated by most skin types, including those with sensitive skin.

  • How often should I use vitamin C?

    Darden says a slow start is best if you're starting a vitamin C serum for the first time or adding a new vitamin C serum you haven't used before. Begin by using a small amount once a day, three or four days a week, and work your way up to a daily application. "Vitamin C is a very active ingredient, and it can cause irritation," she explains. "Introducing it slowly will allow time for your skin to acclimate to the product without causing any irritation. Additionally, adding the product in slowly and by itself will make it easier to pinpoint how well it works for your skin. If you're adding in too many products at the same time, it can make it difficult to understand whether or not a product is working." 

  • When should I apply vitamin C serum?

    The best time to apply vitamin C serum depends on what other products you’re using as well as your skin sensitivity level. Learning how to layer products and when to use them is a science, but once you get your routine down, it’s super simple. Chung encourages her patients with sensitive skin to use vitamin C serum in the morning, but you can also apply it at night with retinol. If your skin isn’t prone to sensitivity, the combination of vitamin C and retinol may give you faster results. If you are susceptible to irritation, use a vitamin C serum in the morning and a retinol at night.
