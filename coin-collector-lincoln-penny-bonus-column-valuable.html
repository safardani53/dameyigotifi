Im a coin collector - your Lincoln penny could be worth up to $2,900, look for the 'bonus' column

July 09, 2024
blog

CRACK open your piggy banks to search for a 1994 penny worth almost $2,900.

Coin collecting isn't just a fun hobby, it can also be very lucrative.

Collectors can get thousands of dollars for coins that most people wouldn't look twice at.

Subtle differences among coins can result in big bucks for those who can spot them.

Valuable pennies, dimes, quarters and nickles could be sitting in your junk drawer right now and you'd never know.

You may even have a rare 1994 penny that sold for $2,875 in 2010.

Read more on rare coins

What to look for

The 1994 penny has an extra column stamped on the far right of the Lincoln Memorial.

This can be tricky to spot with the naked eye, but in this TikTok from Blake Alma of CoinHub he shows how to spot the thin line between the last two columns.

This doubling effect, known as a double die, is an error seen on many valuable coins.

Most read in Money

Coin dies - the metal pieces used to imprint emblems on coins - typically strike coins multiple times to ingrain the image or text.

Sometimes these dies have errors that cause the letters, numbers, and images on some coins to look as if they were stamped on twice in slightly different spots.

More valuable double die coins

The 1955 Lincoln doubled die obverse is one of the most valuable and notorious coins created in the US.

On the face side, "Liberty," "In God We Trust," and the mint year are all very clearly doubled, so 1955 obverse coins are easy to spot.

In an uncirculated state, these coins can sell for up to $17,057.

Morgan silver dollars are valuable without errors but can get you even more cash if there is one.

This variation of the 1901 silver dollar is known as the "Shifted Eagle," because of the doubling on the coin's reverse side.

"In God We Trust" is doubled as well.

USA Coin Book prices circulated 1901 silver dollars between $318 and $2,141 and up to $5,133 in uncirculated condition.

Earlier this year, 1901 Shifted Eagle coins were sold on eBay for $1,950 and $1,632.

One of the rarest error coins, PCGS estimates just 40 doubled die 1917 pennies have survived since the coin was produced 105 years ago.

In uncirculated condition, 1917 doubled die pennies can sell for $1,812, and have a floor of around $250.

One uncirculated doubled die sold for just shy of $15,000 in 2010, according to PCGS.

More recently, an ungraded circulated 1917 doubled die was sold on eBay for $1,602.
