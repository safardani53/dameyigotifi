Burger King to bring back popular sandwich in days fans say it needs to be a permanent nation

March 04, 2024
blog

BURGER King will be bringing back a fan-favorite burger next week that was previously discontinued.

Although it will only be available at select stores, some fans want it to become a permanent menu item nationwide.

Burger King is bringing back the angry Whopper next week at select locations.

Customers at participating restaurants in Columbus, Ohio, can order the previously discontinued sandwich from September 28.

First introduced as a limited-edition item in 2009, the angry Whopper is a spicy take on the chain’s most famous burger.

As well as the usual quarter-pound flame-grilled beef patty, it features pepper jack cheese, crispy bacon, spicy onion tanglers, pickled jalapeños, and angry sauce.

Burger King fans on Reddit have been sharing their excitement over the new menu addition.

One commenter even demanded: “Needs to be a permanent nationwide item.”

Another agreed: “I don’t even like spicy, but this burger was great! Please bring it back nationally."

A third said: “This is the only item that would get me to step foot in a Burger King.”

While Burger King has no plans to reintroduce the angry Whopper nationwide, it is currently giving customers the choice to bring back another spicy menu item.

Burger King Royal Perks members can vote via the app to bring back either the Ghost Pepper Whopper or Ghost Pepper chicken fries.

Both are spicy takes on the chain’s most popular items, but fewer customers have ever tried the Ghost Pepper chicken fries.

The menu item was only previously available during a limited test run in 2021.

COMING HOME

Burger King is also helping customers celebrate the start of the school year with a brand new Homecoming meal.

Available now for a limited time, the combo includes a bunch of the chain’s most beloved items for just $10.

Included in the Home Coming meal are two Whopper Jr. sandwiches, two milkshakes, one small order of onion rings, and one small order of French fries.

What’s more, customers in select cities will even get two paper crowns thrown in.
