Ellen DeGeneres boasted she made one of her employees cry on her show and said it felt good

February 07, 2024
blog

FANS slammed Ellen DeGeneres after a tweet of hers from 2009 about making an employee “cry” resurfaced on Tuesday.

The talk show host, 62, has come under fire over last couple of months after employees came forward claiming she runs a “toxic” work environment.

After weeks of fans and people in the industry, alike, taking sides, either in support of or against, in the Ellen scandal, her old tweet was brought back into the spotlight.

On June 5, 2009, the comedian wrote: “I made one of my employees cry like a baby on today’s show. Honestly, it felt good.”

Twitter users began resharing the post, saying her “mean” behavior was there “in plain sight.”

Three producers from Ellen’s show were fired on Monday after an internal investigation was launched in July by the show's parent company WarnerMedia.

Executive producers Ed Glavin and Kevin Leman, and co-executive producer Jonathan Norman, were are let go in the wake of allegations of racial insensitivity, sexual misconduct and bullying.

People have been calling for Ellen to be fired as well, especially after seeing this resurfaced tweet.

“Can we fire her too please,” one asked, while retweeting her post.

Others said her tweet really “did not age well” and they even thought it was a “parody accounty” until they realized it was from the official @TheEllenShow handle.

The real story behind the tweet, though, has a nicer twist to it, though.

According to Page Six, the tweet was in reference to Ellen Show employee Jeannie Klisiewicz, who was surprised with a cruise during that day’s broadcast.

A clip of Ellen surprising the staffer, who had just joined the company as a receptionist the year prior in 2008, with the large gift shows Jeannie breaking down in tears.

The woman was a big fan of the talk show and wrote to them about wanting a job, which Ellen later talked to her on the phone about on-air before then having her on the show in person to officially offer her a gig.

She was hired to work at the front desk and a year later was gifted a cruise on-air because Ellen wanted to “do something special for her” because of her wonderful attitude.

At first, Ellen joked that she was letting the employee go, before saying she was just teasing and was actually giving her a cruise vacation, which led to the excited staffer crying and jumping up and down.

The resurfaced social media post comes just a day after Ellen apologized again to staffers during a video call for the show’s alleged “toxic” environment and to say she had no idea how the rumor about her not allowing eye contact got started.

According to the The Hollywood Reporter, Ellen said: “I care about each and every one of you. I am grateful for each and every one of you.

"I feel like I’ve kind of let the ball drop a bit because I’m focused on the show, I go in and I do the show, and I’ve just let everybody to do their jobs – to run different departments.

"And it just became a well-oiled machine, and I think that is the problem. It’s not a machine. This is people. These are human beings that are working hard every single day to put this together.

"This show would not be what it is without all of you. It’s become the show that it is because of all of you. So if we have forgotten you somehow – any of us – I’m sorry."
