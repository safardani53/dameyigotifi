A TEENAGE racehorse trainer left a note to his parents saying he had "lived the dream but all good dreams come to an end" - just hours before he is believed to have turned a shotgun on himself.

Tragic Tim Jones, 17, was found with horrific gunshot wounds in the living room of his family home in York last month.

He had been working at the racing stables of trainer Micky Hammond in North Yorks., and was described as a "key part" of the trainer's staff.

But dad Simon, 55, claims Tim had doubts over whether he could make it as a professional racehorse trainer.

He revealed that Tim sent him a heartbreaking last text message which simply read: "I'm sorry dad."

Simon later rang home to be told by a police officer that mum Sally, 52, had found Tim dead in the living room.

He said: "He left a note, Tim was many things but he wasn't a philosopher. He put on this note I have lived the dream, but all good dreams come to an end.

"It was almost as if he knew what he wanted to do but instantly he thought it was probably not achievable. Whether that was grumbling away or not I don't know."

Simon, who has an older son Christopher, 20, has launched a fundraising drive in Tim's memory, to help the charity Racing Welfare build its mental health wellbeing programme.

The fund has already raised more than £10,000 in Tim's memory.

"If I can stop one person doing what Tim did, and one dad going through what I've gone through, then I've done something good.

"I was in France and thought it was strange. I messaged him back and then about 6pm my wife came home from work and walked into the living and there he was in the living room.

"The trauma of that, she went into what can I do to save him mood. It was complete disbelief. Even now, it's almost surreal. Even now, you think he is going to turn up.

"I said to my wife, if he'd had an illness or if he'd had a crash you would think I get why he is not here, but with this, you just don't get it."

An inquest into Tim's death is due to open in the coming months.
